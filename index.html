Compliance

Licensing, Scope, and Label Compliance

Dustin’s Pest Control LLC provides pesticide applications within the scope of Kentucky Category 03 (Lawn, Turf & Ornamental). All applications are performed strictly according to pesticide label directions and applicable Kentucky requirements.

Scope Statement

Services are limited to lawns, turf, and ornamental/landscape vegetation applications under Category 03. This website does not advertise structural pest control services (Category 7A) or public health vector control programs.

The pesticide label is the law. Treatment sites, rates, and methods are limited to label-permitted uses for the product applied. Records are maintained consistent with KDA recordkeeping expectations.

Tip: For the strongest compliance posture, update any promo graphics that mention “mosquito,” “harborage,” “fogging,” “public health,” “eliminate,” or “guaranteed control.” Keep marketing language aligned to Category 03 lawn/turf/ornamental application scope.

FAQ

Common Questions

What can you do under Kentucky Category 03?

Category 03 covers pesticide applications to lawns, turf, and ornamental/landscape vegetation (including shrubs, plantings, and similar non-crop areas), limited to label-permitted uses for the product selected.

Do you offer structural/indoor pest control or termite work?

No. This business is focused on Category 03 lawn/turf/ornamental applications and does not advertise or offer structural pest control (Category 7A), indoor pest programs, or termite services.

How do you decide what to apply?

We consider the target site, conditions, and your goals, then select an approach consistent with label directions and Category 03 scope. The label governs the site, rate, timing, and method.

How often do treatments need to be done?

Frequency depends on the issue, seasonality, weather, and site conditions. Many customers choose a seasonal schedule for consistent results. We’ll recommend a plan based on your property and target sites.

Do you have reduced-impact options?

When appropriate and available, reduced-impact products and methods may be offered—always consistent with label requirements and the application site.
